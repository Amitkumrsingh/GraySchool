Law As a Career in India

Law has always been one of the popular career choices in our country for a long time. Students who come from a lawyers family usually start their career journey in law too. However, it is not mandated to have a family background to become a lawyer or for you to belong to an affluent family. Anyone can study law as long as they have passion for it.

Traditionally, candidates could specialize in either criminal or civil laws. However, this concept has developed with time and now, students opt from various options available for them like corporate laws, patent laws, etc. A degree in law also opens up career options in sectors like legal services, corporate management and administrative services. Students who like a law degree can only be helpful for you to practice as a lawyer in the course or in the company, drop that thought off and get a new perspective.

Students interested in making a career in law can either do a three-year law course after graduation in any discipline or a 5 years course after 12th class. However, the 5-year course is considered a better option than the 3 years degree course in law. In most of the cases, the colleges have 3 years course only students who have their main discipline in graduation other law or it is open for working professionals who want to do an LLB as an additional degree. The 5-year course is strongly recommended for those who want to take up law as a career- be it a legal service or litigator. It is best suited for you.

It is always seen as the best initiation to start off early, a law aspirant is recommended to start preparing for the LAW Entrance exam conducted at the national level for 5 years BA/ BLL at various law schools in India after the secondary exam. The national law entrance exams and state law exams like CLAT(Common Law Admission Test) and LAWCET(AP/TS) are commonly conducted to test the student's General English, legal Aptitude, logical skills, General Awareness, etc. Some law colleges offer three years' LLB courses which have a similar syllabus.

Is LAW Career the best fit for me?

Law Career requires loads of logical skills and patience. It takes a good amount of dedication and hard work to become a successful lawyer in India. Without a doubt, the first generation lawyers must have faced many problems just like in any other profession. For newbies, it takes time, dedication and constant efforts, with positive determination, you can own your law cases and your law career. To become a successful lawyer, great communication skills, good articulation and critical analysis should be a daily thing. You should analyze these points before you take up law as a career. If you need help with it, you can always join one of the CLAT Coaching Centres Near You to help you enhance these skills and get you to the clear LAW Entrance Exams.

Funding and scholarship

There are not many law schools that offer financial help on the basis of the entrance exam. The student should communicate with authorities and find out about the scholarship policies with respect to the universities. Students can always opt for a bank loan or apply for various scholarships that are available from time to time.

Job Opportunities for a Lawyer

No need to have a second thought, there are numerous job opportunities for law graduates. You can either practice as an advocate in a court of law or work with corporate companies as a legal advisor. You can become a judge too, how? After clearing exams conducted by the Public Service Commission. After gaining good experience, a law graduate can become a public prosecutor, solicitor general or offer services to government departments and ministries. You can also choose to work as a legal advisor for various organizations. Other options may include, teaching in colleges, working as a reporter, and working with NGOs.

Salary Package after Law Graduation

When you start practising in the court, you may get a stipend of Rs 5000 to Rs 40,000 depending on the advocate you are associated with, a law graduate working with legal process outsourcing may receive a salary from 20,000 up to 50,000. It is a very high paying profession but truly depends on the candidate's calibre, success and popularity.

What are the demand and supply like?

There has always been a shortage of qualified lawyers in India. The Bar Council of India expressed its concern that young lawyers do join the bar and nearly ten lakh lawyers in India are available but only 20 percent of them are considered fit to practice law in courts. There are several law schools in India like NLSIU and NALSAR which were established to up the level of legal education to produce skilled lawyers to meet the requirement and have many law graduates to practice law in the court.

Scope of Law Career in Abroad

Legal education in India is as good as the one outside India. However, there are several universities in the UK which offer legal education to Indian students. To my knowledge, Indian students quite enjoy studying and working in the UK wherein they receive good salary packages. A recent study has shown that students from India who study in the USA in LAW Colleges receive decent jobs and an attractive salary package after graduation. Some of the International Law Schools are HARVARD Law school, Yale University, Australia National University, etc.

Pros and Cons of Law Career

Lawyers, just like other professionals such as chartered accountant, doctors etc require experience to polish and enhance their skills and become valuable and best for their clients. Therefore, time is crucial and it helps you become the best of the best lawyers. Candidates who are determined to succeed in this profession will have to gather up lots of patience in the initial phase of their career. However, there is no limitation on money, success, growth, power and satisfaction as a lawyer when there is justice served.

What are the career roles as a Lawyer?

1. Criminal lawyer :

A lawyer who specializes in criminal laws IPC, Evidence Act and other penal laws.

2. Civil Litigation lawyer :

A lawyer who specializes in civil laws, for example, taxation laws, excise laws.


3. Legal Analyst:

The one who works for corporate firms, law firms and analyses the laws with respect to the firm and its operations.

4. Document Drafting Lawyer:

This kind of lawyer specializes in drafting various documents containing terms and conditions, agreements, case material, etc.

5. Legal Advisor:

Offers consultancy to companies regarding their legal duties, legal relations, obligations with other firms.

6. Legal Journalist :

It covers legal procedures, crime beats, arbitration courts, arbitration events and international courts.

7. Government Lawyer:

The lawyer here works closely with the government and coordination with the police.

8. Judge:

The judge offers judgement after conducting court proceedings and hearing from all the concerned parties.

A few tips to get hired

Working on what you have been reading and dreaming all your life is the best part. Candidates who have finished their five years' LLB from Top law schools like NALSAR Hyderabad, NLSUI Bangalore can get attractive job opportunities from law firms and ITES firms. Many opt to start their own practice. All the law graduates who would like to practice in courts to get themselves registered in the Bar Council of India(BCI) and practice under senior lawyers.
Practical training in court can improve your career chances of getting hired by law firms and reputed lawyers. Though most of the law colleges impart practical legal education to final year law students, it is important that you should take initiative and start visiting court proceedings in the final year to understand the day-to-day affair and to get best at the practice.

How can you become a lawyer in India?

If you are still in 11th or 12th grade, you can take the Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) or take a 5 year B.A LLA course from one of the 16 National Law universities (NLUs).
If you are a graduate, you can start off with a 3-year law course. However, note that none of the NLUs provides a 3-year law course.
