Frequently asked questions for CLAT exam

1What is CLAT Exam?
CLAT or Common Law Admission Test is a centralized exam conducted for law aspirants every year for law entrance admissions to 22 National law Universities (NLUs). The CLAT Entrance exam is generally conducted on the 2nd Sunday of May for the students trying to get into the Integrated Five Year Law undergraduate course at the National Law Universities(NLUs).

Talking about the CLAT Exam pattern, the CLAT Exam consists of 5 broad sections namely: English, General Knowledge, Elementary Maths, Current Affairs, Logical Reasoning and Legal Aptitude. The candidates are required to solve 150 questions in 120 minutes distributed across all the 5 sections.
2 What type of questions are asked in the CLAT exam?
CLAT exam consists of 200 questions in total which are further divided into sections such as English comprehension - 40 questions, GK and current affairs - 50 questions, Numerical Ability - 20 questions, Legal aptitude - 50 questions and logical reasoning - 40 questions. The kind of questions asked in the CLAT exam generally come from CLAT Syllabus.

4What is CLAT paper?
CLAT is abbreviated into Common Law Test which is conducted all India for people aspiring to pursue law courses such as LLB (Legum Baccalaureus) and LLM(Legum Magister). If you are law aspirant, CLAT exam is best for your after 12th grade.

8 Is that sectional cut off will be there in 2019 clat exam?
No, CLAT exam does not have any such minimum cut off score or sectional cut off to clear CLAT exam. The score is calculated over all and purely calculated based on the candidate's performance.
9Is it possible to change preference list after CLAT exam but before the results are announced?
No, the preference list cannot be changed once it is submitted during the time of the registration as the allocation of NLUs work based on ‘merit-cum-preference’ which means that your preference list will be used to allocated a NLU seat.
