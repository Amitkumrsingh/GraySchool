About Common Law Admission Test (CLAT)

Common Law Admission Test (CLAT) Exam is the most preferred law entrance exam in India. CLAT exam was started in 2008 and more than 40,000 students write CLAT today. CLAT is conducted on a rotational basis to select the eligible students for the 19 National Law Universities in India, excluding National Law University, Delhi and HP National Law University, Shima as they have their own selection process.

All the nineteen National Law Universities offer a five-year UG law Programmes and most of the NLUs also offer PG Programmes in Law. NUALS, Kochi is organizing the Eleventh Common Law Admission Test (CLAT 2018). Other Law schools like NMIMS Mumbai, Nirma University Ahmedabad, and UPES Dehradun also accept the CLAT score.

Eligibility Criteria for CLAT exam (UG and PG programmes)

UG Programme eligibility for CLAT exam:

1. Minimum percentage of marks in the qualifying exam (i.e., 10+2), the candidate must secure:
45% marks in case the candidate belongs to Unreserved/OBC or any other categories
40% marks in case the candidate belongs to SC/ST categories.

2. The candidate appearing for CLAT exam should submit their result of qualifying exam (i.e., 10+2) failing at which the candidate shall be marked ineligible for the admission process.

3. The break of the tie due to equal marks in CLAT exam shall be done by the following process:
Higher marks secured in the legal aptitude in the CLAT exam
Higher age
Computerized draw

PG Programme eligibility for CLAT exam:

1. The candidate must have minimum pass percentage in the qualifying examination (i.e. LL.B/Five ear Integrated LL.B. (Hons.)/or any other equivalent exam) as below:
55% marks in case the candidate belongs to Unreserved/OBC or any other categories
50% marks in case the candidate belongs to SC/ST categories.

2. The candidate must have passed or appeared in the final semester/ final year exam on the date of his/her admission.

3. The candidate has to submit the final result of qualifying exam within the prescribed time frame by the respective University or institute.

4. The break of the tie due to equal marks in CLAT exam shall be done by the following process:
Higher marks secured in the legal aptitude in the CLAT exam
Higher age
Computerized draw


CLAT Syllabus

CLAT exam paper would have 200 questions, each from 5 different subjects such as English, General Knowledge and current affairs, Elementary mathematics/ Numerical Ability, Legal Aptitude and Logical Reasoning.


CLAT Exam Pattern

The duration of CLAT exam is 2 hours, it comprises of 200 questions as below:

Sections No. of questions
English Language 40
General knowledge & current affairs 50
Elementary Mathematics/Numerical Ability 20
Legal Aptitude 50
Logical Reasoning 40

English

English section in CLAT exam comprises of questions based on reading comprehension, the passage, the grammar, filling in the blanks with appropriate words, correction of incorrect grammatical sentences, meanings of words from comprehension.

General knowledge and Current Affairs

The general knowledge section in the CLAT exam test the general awareness of the candidate using the questions on current affairs about the national and international current affairs.

Mathematics

This section in the CLAT exam tests the elementary mathematics i.e., the math taught to the candidate up to 10th grade.

Legal Aptitude

Legal Aptitude section in CLAT exam tests the candidate’s interest in the study of law, problem solving ability, research aptitude. Some questions may include legal propositions.

Logical reasoning

This section includes questions on syllogisms, analogies and analogies. The candidate’s ability to identify the logical links, patterns and illogical arguments is also tested.
