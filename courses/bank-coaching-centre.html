About Bank Exams

In Indian Economy, banking is considered as one of the fastest growing industry. More branches of the banks are being opened in every part of the country as the government is trying to provide banking facilities in every village and town, Under the financial Inclusion scheme.

Here are the following levels for recruitment in the bank sector:

Bank Probationary Officer (Bank PO): A managerial position at the bank. After 1-2 years of training, the candidates are assigned the position of Assistant Managers (AM) or Deputy Managers (DM). The starting salary ranges from RS. 30,000 to RS. 40,000 depending on the bank and the location of the posting.

Bank clerks: A clerical position at the bank and role of the job is considered as the entry-level job in the bank. Salary ranges from RS. 20,000 - 25, 000 depending on the bank and the location of the posting.

Specialist Officers (SO): This hiring is done by banks for profiles like - HR officer, IT officer, Marketing Officer, Finance Officer, Agriculture Officer, Law Officer, etc. Usually, the salary for Specialist Officers is same as the Probationary Officers.

The employment in the banking sector has a wide range of possibility for a government job as Government banks recruit more than 1,00,000 candidates every year.

For the recruitment of Bank PO, Bank Clerk & Specialist officers, multiple exams are conducted every year. The following categories are how these exams can be classified into:-



IBPS PO Coaching in Hyderabad

This is an exam conducted by Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS) for 20 PSU Bank for the recruitment. IBPS Clerk: This is also an exam conducted by Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS) for 20 PSU Banks for the hiring of Clerical Cadres.

IBPS Specialist Officers (IBPS SO)

Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS) for 20 PSU Banks for the hiring of Specialist Officers like IT officer, Marketing Officer, HR officer etc.

IBPS Regional Rural Bank Exam

This exam is conducted by Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS) for all the Regional Rural Banks of India (RRBs) for Probationary Officer, Specialist Officers, and Clerical Officer posts.

SBI Probationary Officer

This exam is conducted by State Bank of India to recruit Probationary Officers.

SBI Clerk

This exam is conducted by State Bank of India to recruit Clerical Cadres.

RBI Grade B Exam

Through all India Exam, Reserve Bank of India recruits officers (Grade B). Although there are limited jobs in this, RBI has its own benefits and growth for its own employees.

RBI Assistants Exam

Through all India Exam, Reserve Bank of India recruits Clerical Cadres.

Syllabus for Bank Exams

The bank exam syllabus includes written tests to examine the general Aptitude of the candidates. Each section has 100 questions - The sections are Quantitative Aptitude with 35 Questions, Reasoning Ability with 35 questions each, English Language with 30 questions each.

Syllabus for each section is as follows :

  • Quantitative Aptitude:
    Data Interpretation, Time and Distance, Number Systems, Ratio and Proportion, Profit and Loss, Simple Interest & Compound Interest, Percentage and Averages, Surds and Indices, Mensuration- Cone, Sequence and Series, Cylinder, Sphere, Quadratic Equations, Permutation Combination, and Probability.
  • Reasoning Ability :
    SCoding-Decoding, Non-Verbal Reasoning, Alphanumeric series, Sitting Arrangements, Input-Output, Tabulation, Data Sufficiency, Direction, Alphabet test, Ranking. Code Inequalities.
  • English Language :
    Sentence correction, Vocabulary, Multiple meaning words, Read Comprehension, Paragraph completion, Cloze test.
  • Computers :
    Computer basics, Hardware, Software, Database, Networking, Internet (History, Concept, Application), Virus, Number system, History of Computers, Logic Gates, MS office, Security tools.
  • Current Affairs:
    Latest news about Banks, Bussiness NEWS, Books and Authors, Summits, National and Internation, Visits, Committees, Government Schemes, Sports, Obituaries, Defense, Reports and Indexes, Economy based current affairs.
  • Banking: :
    Types of Accounts, Financial Inclusion, Money market instruments, Banking Abbreviations, RBI and its functions, Banking Regulation Act 1949, Negotiable Instruments Act 1881, Capital market, Priority sector lending, Banking OMBUDSMAN scheme 2006
