About Polytechnic Common Entrance Test (POLYCET)

The Polytechnic entrance exam is conducted every year by the State Board of Technical Education and Training. This exam is conducted annually in the month of April to screen out the most eligible candidates aspiring for admissions into the engineering diploma courses. It is otherwise known as POLYCET exam. The diploma level programs are available at Aided, Private, and Government polytechnics and Unaided polytechnics run in Private unaided Engineering colleges. For diploma courses like Catering Technology, Hotel Management, and D. Pharmacy, the candidates should have passed 12th grade and need not appear for POLYCET exam.



Eligibility Criteria for Polytechnic Entrance Exam

All the candidates appearing for POLYCET exam need to qualify the eligibility criteria, although the eligibility criteria varies from one state level to another. The basic criteria to appear in the Polytechnic Entrance Exam are as follows:


Polytechnic entrance exam syllabus

POLYCET exam syllabus will mostly comprise of questions from the academic syllabus i.e., SSC syllabus that includes subjects such as Physics, Chemistry, and mathematics.

Candidate's nationality should be Indian

Candidate should obtain minimum of 35% marks in the qualifying exam

Candidate appearing for POLYCET exam should compulsorily have mathematics as one of the subjects in the education curriculum.

For Polytechnic diploma, the candidate should have passed 10th or any equivalent examination in order to be eligible for admissions. There can be a few courses that may require candidates to have 12th passed for the admissions.

For lateral entry, the candidates who have passed 12th examination can apply for polytechnic courses.


Polytechnic Entrance Exam Pattern

The POLYCET exam is pen and paper-based with objective type questions of around 120 each. The question paper is published in English and Telugu and no negative marks are deducted for an incorrect answer. Check out the Polytechnic entrance exam pattern in detail as follows:

Subject No. of questions
Physics 30
Chemistry 30
Mathematics 60
Total 120
