Tips to crack Banking Exams with a Good score

With the year almost touching its quarter, all the bank exam aspirants still have some important banking and insurance exams coming up such as IBPS PO Mains, IBPS Clerk, IBPS SO, FCI Manager and many more. It is important to gear up your patience, energy, sacrifice, speed, fight, hard work and your preparation for Bank exams.

The questions asked in bank exams are getting trickier with each day. Like you are aware, according to the statistics only 10% out of 100% candidates clear the final selection. It is time to put up hard work and perseverance to crack bank exams with good scores.

Even before you consider writing Bank exams, you need to answer questions like what is your aim? What do you want to do if you clear the bank exam? Once you are aware of why you want to write bank exams, you can easily focus on bank exam preparation with any barriers.

How to crack Bank Exams?

We believe, everything is possible with proper preparation and planning to crack banking exams.

Before you start your preparation, sit down and prepare an approach to analysis where you stand before your bank exam preparation. That is, which section are you good at and which section are you bad at? What areas do you need improvement? Can you prepare for the exam on your own or do you need help from the Bank Exam coaching centre near you? How much time do you need to practice enough? After you have done answering these questions, make sure you take an online mock test and measure your score. This makes it easy for you to prepare well for your exams.

Remember, while taking the test, take it seriously as though you are answering questions from the real one. Once you are done, note your score and see the pattern of your result and improve in areas where you need improvements.

Parameters you need to focus on which preparing for Banking exams

1. Good speed, Good Accuracy:

You are doing good but need more practice.

2. Good speed, Bad Accuracy:

Check out your answers and see where you are going wrong and prepare well on those topics.

3. Bad Speed, Bad Accuracy:

A person who stands in this parameter needs more practice, you will need more focus and research on easy tips and techniques to crack a bank exam.


4. Bad Speed, Good Accuracy:

Practice, practice, practice. Practice every question like your entire exam score depends on that one question. Try and apply shortcuts to improve your speed and accuracy.


Once you have made this assessment based on the parameters above, learn more about the bank exam, the exam pattern and the topics the bank exam involves like Mathematics, English, reasoning, logical reasoning, Data Interpretation, computer and general awareness.

Tips to master English section in Bank Exams

1.Improve your context skills and learn how to master the difficult words.
2.Play word games online to improve your word game for the exam
3.Practise vocabulary tests and improve your results each time.
4.Get excited about works, read more books, blogs, websites, newspapers for varying topics and length.
5.This will surely improve your reading speed and comprehension.

Tips to master Quantitative section

1.Get your fundamentals strong by referring to all the quantitative material.
2.Improve the ability to perform simple and complex calculations under pressure and use the logic and reasoning techniques to solve all the math questions.
3.In any competitive exam, the pacing is an important factor, it involves the practice of moving into another question if you are not able to finish the current question you are stuck with.

Tips to master Reasoning section

This section helps you solve complex situations and most importantly, it reflects your ability to solve your complex situations and your approach. This section requires a lot of practice. You need to practice more questions on reasoning.

Tips to master GK section

1.General Awareness acts as a game-changer in competitive exams. This section needs vast information with daily updates about current awareness.
2.Make a habit of reading the newspaper every day.
3.Keep an eye on what's happening around and in the world.
4.Read leading magazines that focus on general knowledge and current affairs.
5.Make a habit of reading articles every day.
6.Keep revising the current affairs every day to keep track of all the current affairs in the world.

General Tips to crack Bank Exam with a good score

1.Before you start your preparation, know the Bank exams syllabus and exam pattern.
2.Create a study plan for every subject based on the bank exam syllabus.
3.Use the best resource possible for Bank Exam Preparation from a trusted source.
4.Joining a Bank Coaching centre near you can be a good option.
5.Identify and work on weak areas.
6.Focus on all the sections equally.
7.Practice with previous papers and mock tests.
8.Use online material for the bank exam for preparation.
9.Make a list of books to prepare for Bank Exams.
10.Check frequent and important online to heat up the preparation.
11.Evaluate your speed and accuracy.
12.Keep in par with all the current affairs.
13.Stay calm and focused.
14.Take a break when needed.
15.Make sure to not do the guessing game in the exam.
16.Start with the topic you are most confident about the exam.
